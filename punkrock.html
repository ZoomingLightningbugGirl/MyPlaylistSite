Playlist Time!

Here are some of my favorite Punk-Rock songs that I put into a playlist...

  • Green Day - "American Idiot": Let's be real here. This is 100% their most iconic song. As an Idiot Nation's national anthem, it serves as an identifying proof of the denial of typical American oblivion under politics. It carries a natural empowerment that makes you want to get up, protest, and be what makes you unique from others.
  • Green Day - "Basket Case": I love this one for its role in my childhood. I listened to it a lot growing up, and it always elevates my mood!
  • Awolnation - "Passion": A strong, unapologetic anthem of pursuing what makes you, you.
  • Awolnation - "Sail": An admission to one's shortcomings, it just has some sort of ingredient that makes you want to fight for your right to be seen in all your fallouts.
  • Awolnation - "The Best": With a fun MV and catchy chorus, it DEFINITELY makes me want to "walk a little bit taller". This was the song I found at eight years old that brought me into the AWOL fandom.
  • Moonwalker HQ - "New God": A calling-out to the harsh political and scientific control of the Church, this authentic indie punk-rock artist inspires people to rethink what it means to be spiritually connected to one another in a poetry-like lyrical fashion.
  • Green Day - "Jesus of Suburbia": If you can't tell, Green Day is a pretty popular tenant living rent-free in my Spotify. Though Jesus of Suburbia is sometimes a tad too long for my "dingaling TikTok brain" (quote from my bio teacher), it still has a delightful melody that puts me in an upbeat mood. I'd totally recommend it for people who like longer songs with a similar vibe through the whole thing so you don't have to keep replaying it. Because it is TOTALLY replay-worthy.
