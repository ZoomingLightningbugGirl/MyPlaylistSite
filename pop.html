Playlist Time!

Here are some of my favorite Pop songs that I put into a playlist...

  • Adéla - "Superscar" This diva-coded Slovakian girl from Dream Academy was destined to fail that survival show— so she could succeed in her own unique solo career. In fact, I should feel so lucky to listen to this strong, feministic speech of vulnerability to others.
  • Addison Rae - "Fame is a Gun": With her "tone of innocence", renowned internet superstar Addison Rae drops a self-confidence hymn to living your truth even if it may put others at unease with your feminist confidence. As fame "is a gun" and she "points it blind", she paints a picture of her iconic reputation that she uses to bring people up and "shoot down" the haters. She even got nominated for a grammy. So, props to her for her iconicness across socials.
  • Zara Larsson - "Lush Life": As a Friday-night anthem, "Lush Life" grants a sort of serenity yet energy in a person that makes you feel accomplished for all you've lived through so far. It also evokes an appreciation for the world around you.
  • Mad Tsai - "Catch these Strays": A cute song that puts you in a great mood with its catchy tune and playful vibe.
  • Lady Gaga ft. Beyoncé - "Telephone": Okay, this one straight-up HAS to take the cake as my favorite Kpop song OAT. With its chaotic MV and feminist boss energy, it quickly became one of my favorite Kpop/Rap songs. And it's NOT just because my bias is the rapper queen ASA...
  • KATSEYE - "Touch": Let's be real. That 'little bit of everything, whole lotta energy' line must have had SOMETHING in it with how addictive it is. Because of course you won't get ahead of me, I'm literally 100 miles per hour with a whole lotta energy!
  • KATSEYE - "Gabriela": You may be starting to see a pattern here— and you'd be right. But Babymonster is just TOO massively majestic, almost like the low taper fade in the massive '26. 'Drip' is what really set them going with their music career, with a catchy instrumental and crazy, unapologetic visuals.
