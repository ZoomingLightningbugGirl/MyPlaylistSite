Playlist Time!

Here are some of my favorite Rap songs that I put into a playlist...

  • Playboi Carti - "Timeless" To be honest, I've never been much of a Playboi Carti fan. However, I got into the Arcane fandom in November of 2025, and I saw the most insane edit of Powder's transition into Jinx— all set to this song. With a smooth melody and catchy melody "ever since I was a chip, I been legit", this song was revolutionary to me in 2025.
  • Kendrick Llamar - "Not Like Us": Kendrick really dropped a dime on Drake, because that man did NOT disrespect Pac after this aggressive diss track. With his call-outs to Drake's submission to corporal ladders for money, as well as his remarks about his unhealthy relationship desires, this beat makes you want to stomp your feat to the music and scream out those creative insults at a capitalist.
  • Kendrick Llamar - "TV off": This one is hella iconic and a quintessential tribute to his offensive yet authentic rap style. It truly contributes to his distinct rap identity and shows his cultural awareness in the contemporary world.
  • Eminem - "Godzilla": A cute song that puts you in a great mood with its catchy tune and playful vibe.
  • Eminem - "Cinderella Man": Okay, this one straight-up HAS to take the cake as my favorite Kpop song OAT. With its chaotic MV and feminist boss energy, it quickly became one of my favorite Kpop/Rap songs. And it's NOT just because my bias is the rapper queen ASA...
  • Kendrick Llamar - "Humble": Let's be real. That 'little bit of everything, whole lotta energy' line must have had SOMETHING in it with how addictive it is. Because of course you won't get ahead of me, I'm literally 100 miles per hour with a whole lotta energy!
  • Eminem - "Lose Yourself": You may be starting to see a pattern here— and you'd be right. But Babymonster is just TOO massively majestic, almost like the low taper fade in the massive '26. 'Drip' is what really set them going with their music career, with a catchy instrumental and crazy, unapologetic visuals.
